# Technical Guide for AI Video Generation: Quinzhee Construction

## Topic: Inserting Depth-Guide Sticks for Uniform Wall Thickness

This guide outlines the physical process of inserting depth-guide sticks into a quinzhee snow mound. Adherence to these details will ensure a realistic and mechanically accurate depiction of this crucial survival task.

### 1. Tools and Materials

#### **Primary Tool: Depth-Guide Sticks**

*   **Visual Appearance:**
    *   A collection of 15-25 straight, sturdy sticks.
    *   **Length:** Each stick should be uniformly 12 to 18 inches (30 to 45 cm) long. Consistency is key for a uniform wall thickness.
    *   **Diameter:** Roughly the thickness of a finger or thumb.
    *   **Material:** Natural, debarked tree branches are most common. Alternatively, wooden dowels or even paint stir sticks can be used for a more improvised look.
*   **How They Are Held:**
    *   The user will hold one stick at a time.
    *   The hand grips the stick firmly near one end, much like holding a large dart. The fingers are wrapped around the stick, with the thumb providing stability.

#### **Secondary Material: The Quinzhee Mound**

*   **Visual Appearance:**
    *   A large, compacted pile of snow, shaped into a dome. A flat or peaked roof is a sign of incorrect construction and should be avoided.
    *   The surface should be somewhat firm and consolidated, not loose powder. This is because the mound has been piled and compacted, and is undergoing a process called sintering (hardening).
    *   The size should be substantial, typically at least 5-7 feet high and 8-10 feet in diameter for a 1-2 person shelter.

### 2. Step-by-Step Physical Execution

This action takes place *after* the snow mound has been fully piled and shaped, but *before* excavation begins. The snow needs to rest and harden (sinter) for at least 90 minutes to a few hours after the sticks are placed.

#### **Step 1: Selecting and Gripping the Stick**

*   **Physical Movement:** The individual kneels or stands beside the snow mound. They reach for their pile of prepared sticks, selecting one. The stick is held in one hand, oriented with the pointed end (if any) facing the snow mound.
*   **Visual Cues:** The snow of the mound should look packed, not fluffy. Footprints or shovel marks from its construction may still be visible.

#### **Step 2: Positioning and Insertion**

*   **Physical Movement:**
    *   The person positions the tip of the stick against the snow mound.
    *   The stick must be aimed **perpendicular to the surface of the dome** at that specific point. This is a critical detail for realism. On the side of the mound, the stick will be inserted horizontally. Near the top, it will be inserted more vertically.
    *   The body movement is a firm, steady push originating from the shoulder and elbow. It is not a violent jab, but a controlled press. The other hand can be placed on the mound for balance.
*   **Visual Cues:** The snow will slightly compact around the entry point of the stick as it is pushed in. There should be minimal cracking or flaking if the snow has been properly prepared.

#### **Step 3: Setting the Depth**

*   **Physical Movement:** The stick is pushed into the mound until a specific length remains exposed.
    *   **Realistic Method 1 (Easier Removal):** Push the stick in until about two inches remain visible on the outside. This allows for easy removal later.
    *   **Realistic Method 2 (Interior-Only Guide):** Push the stick in completely, so that only the very end is visible, or it is flush with the surface. This method makes the sticks purely an internal guide for the person excavating.
*   **Visual Cues:** The final appearance should be a consistent length of stick protruding from the mound (if using Method 1).

#### **Step 4: Systematic Placement**

*   **Physical Movement:** The individual moves around the quinzhee, repeating the insertion process.
    *   Sticks should be placed in a grid-like pattern across the entire surface of the dome, from the base to the top.
    *   **Spacing:** The sticks should be spaced approximately 1 to 2 feet (30 to 60 cm) apart.
*   **Visual Cues:** As more sticks are inserted, the quinzhee mound will begin to resemble a porcupine. The pattern should look deliberate and evenly spaced, not random.

### 3. Mistakes to Avoid (What Looks Fake vs. Real)

| Fake/Incorrect Appearance | Real/Correct Appearance |
| :--- | :--- |
| Sticks are inserted at random, haphazard angles. | Sticks are inserted perpendicular to the curve of the dome at each point. |
| Sticks are only placed around the base or just on top. | The entire surface of the mound is evenly covered with sticks. |
| The snow mound is made of light, fluffy powder. | The snow is visibly packed and consolidated. |
| The person starts digging immediately after placing the sticks. | There is a significant time delay (at least 90 minutes) between placing the sticks and starting to hollow out the shelter to allow the snow to sinter. |
| The sticks are of wildly different lengths. | The sticks are uniform in length, indicating deliberate preparation. |
| The mound has a pointed peak like a cone. | The mound is a smooth, rounded dome for structural integrity. |
| When excavating from the inside, the person ignores the sticks and punches through the wall. | The person stops digging in a specific area as soon as they encounter the tip of a guide stick. |
