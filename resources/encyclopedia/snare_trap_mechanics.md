# SURVIVAL MECHANICS: Wire Snare Trap (For Arctic Hare)

## Core Concept
A passive trap that uses a simple wire loop to catch a small animal (like a hare) around the neck as it runs along a designated path. The forward momentum of the animal causes a slip-knot mechanism to pull tight.

## Visual & Physical Mechanics for Prompt Generation

When generating video prompts involving setting a snare trap, adhere strictly to these mechanical realities:

### 1. The Wire & Loop Mechanics
- **Material:** 22 to 24 gauge wire (copper, brass, or steel). It must be malleable but strong enough to hold its shape. Copper is visually distinctive (reddish-brown).
- **The Knot:** The snare uses a "slip-knot loop". The survivor creates a tiny 1-inch eyelet at one end, twisting the tag end tightly around the main wire 5-6 times to secure it. The other end of the wire is pushed through this eyelet to form the main snare loop.
- **Loop Size:** Visually, the main loop should be about 4 inches (10 cm) in diameter—roughly the size of a human fist.

### 2. Positioning & Height
- **Location:** Placed in an active "run" (a visible trail in the snow packed down by hares).
- **Height above Ground:** The bottom curve of the loop must hover precisely 3 to 4 inches (roughly four fingers' width) above the snow surface. This targets the hare's neck, not its body or legs.

### 3. Anchoring and Support
- **The Anchor:** The long, non-loop end of the wire MUST be securely wrapped multiple times around a very sturdy anchor (a thick tree root, a heavy branch, or a deeply driven stake).
- **The Support:** To keep the wire loop suspended at the correct height in the air, a "Y-shaped" stick (a small forked twig) is often driven into the snow. The wire rests in the notch of the "Y".

### 4. Guiding the Prey (Fencing)
- **Natural Funnel:** The survivor must place dead twigs and brush on either side of the snare to create a V-shaped funnel. This prevents the hare from walking around the loop.
- **The Chin Stick:** A small, thin twig is placed horizontally in the snow directly *under* the loop. This prevents the hare from ducking its head under the wire, forcing it to lift its head slightly and pass straight into the snare.

## Common AI Hallucinations to Prevent
- **Floating Loops:** The loop cannot magically float. It must be anchored to a tree or supported by a stick.
- **Complex Rope Knots:** AI often tries to tie elaborate boy-scout knots with thick rope. It must be thin wire, twisted onto itself, not tied like shoelaces.
- **Wrong Size:** Avoid generating massive loops meant for deer or wolves when targeting a hare. The "fist-sized" rule is key.
