# SURVIVAL MECHANICS: Quinzhee (Snow Dugout Shelter)

## Core Concept
A Quinzhee is a snow shelter made by piling snow, letting it sinter (harden), and hollowing it out. It differs from an igloo because it doesn't use carved blocks of firm snow; it uses loose snow that is piled and compacted.

## Visual & Physical Mechanics for Prompt Generation

When generating video prompts involving a Quinzhee construction, adhere strictly to these mechanical realities:

### 1. The Snow Pile & Sintering
- **Action:** The survivor must pile snow into a large mound, ideally 5 to 8 feet high and wide enough for a person.
- **Tool:** A shovel or an improvised large, flat object (e.g., a curved sheet of aircraft aluminum or a panel).
- **Sintering (Crucial Step):** The pile must sit untouched for 60 to 90 minutes. Do not show the survivor digging immediately after piling. The snow must fuse (sinter).

### 2. Depth Guides (The "Porcupine" Look)
- **Visual Marker:** Before excavating, the survivor must gather sticks about 12 inches (30 cm) long.
- **Action:** They jab these sticks straight into the snow dome from the outside, all over the surface, leaving them flush with the snow exterior.
- **Purpose:** When digging from the inside, hitting the inner tip of a stick tells the survivor the wall is exactly 12 inches thick and they must stop digging to prevent collapse.

### 3. The Excavation & Cold Trap
- **The Tunnel:** The entrance is a narrow, low hole at the base, just wide enough to squeeze shoulders through. The tunnel should angle *downwards* briefly, then back *up* into the main chamber. This creates a "Cold Trap" where dense, cold air settles at the lowest point, away from the sleeping area.
- **The Digging Stance:** The survivor must be shown on their hands and knees, or lying on their side/back, in a highly cramped space.
- **The Motion:** Using a small tool (like a trowel, a piece of metal, or hands), they scrape the compacted interior snow and drag/kick it backward out of the tunnel behind them.

### 4. Interior Architecture
- **Sleeping Platform:** The floor of the main chamber must be elevated above the bottom of the entrance tunnel (the cold trap).
- **The Ceiling:** Must be smoothed into a perfect dome to prevent condensation from dripping; it causes moisture to run down the walls instead.
- **Ventilation:** A small hole (about an inch wide) poked through the roof to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning and suffocation.

## Common AI Hallucinations to Prevent
- **Igloo Blocks:** Ensure the AI doesn't generate neat, rectangular ice blocks. A Quinzhee looks like an organic mound of snow.
- **Instant Digging:** The AI might show piling and digging simultaneously. This disobeys the sintering physics.
- **Standing Up:** The survivor cannot stand up during the digging phase; it is entirely a claustrophobic, crawling process.
