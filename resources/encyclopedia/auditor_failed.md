# Technical Guide for Video Generation AI: Auditor Failed

## Introduction

This document provides a detailed, practical, and physical step-by-step guide for realistically depicting the concept of "Auditor Failed." In a real-world context, this scenario often translates to the successful, undetected bypassing of a tamper-evident security seal. Such seals are single-use mechanisms designed to leave clear visual evidence of any unauthorized access. A "failed audit" in this context means the tampering was so successful that it would pass a typical inspection.

This guide focuses on the physical manipulation of a common plastic indicative seal, often used on utility meters, cargo, and sensitive containers. The goal is to provide the AI with the necessary data to generate a visually accurate and mechanically plausible sequence of events.

## I. Scenario Overview: Bypassing a Plastic Indicative Seal with Heat

This method involves using controlled heat to soften the plastic of the seal's locking mechanism, allowing it to be opened and then re-secured without leaving the typical signs of tampering.

### Key Visual Elements:
*   **Seal Type:** A fixed-length plastic security seal, often with a serial number.
*   **Environment:** A dimly lit or concealed area, suggesting surreptitious activity.
*   **Tools:** A heat gun (or a similar focused heat source), a small metal probe (like a file or precision screwdriver), and optionally, pliers.
*   **Operator:** Hands should appear steady and deliberate. The body posture should be focused and close to the work.

## II. Step-by-Step Physical Breakdown

### Step 1: Initial Inspection and Preparation

The operator carefully examines the seal to understand its locking mechanism. This is a crucial step for a successful bypass.

*   **Physical Movements:**
    *   The operator holds the object to which the seal is attached, rotating it to get a clear view of the seal.
    *   Fingers gently probe the seal, testing its tightness and identifying the locking chamber.
    *   The operator may use a small light source, like a penlight, to illuminate the seal's internal components through the semi-translucent plastic.

*   **Tools and Handling:**
    *   Initially, no tools are used. The focus is on tactile and visual inspection.
    *   The operator's hands are bare or wearing thin gloves to maximize dexterity.

*   **Visual Appearance of Materials:**
    *   The plastic seal is intact, with no stress marks or discoloration.
    *   The serial number is clearly visible and unaltered.

### Step 2: Applying Controlled Heat

The objective is to soften the plastic around the locking mechanism without melting or discoloring it, which would be a clear sign of tampering.

*   **Physical Movements:**
    *   The operator holds the heat gun with their dominant hand, maintaining a distance of several inches from the seal.
    *   The heat is applied in short, even bursts, with the operator constantly moving the heat source to avoid concentrating heat on one spot.
    *   The non-dominant hand holds the object steady, away from the direct heat stream.

*   **Tools and Handling:**
    *   A heat gun is held in a firm, controlled grip. The operator's finger is on the trigger, applying heat intermittently.
    *   The nozzle of the heat gun is aimed specifically at the part of the seal containing the locking teeth.

*   **Visual Appearance of Materials:**
    *   The plastic of the seal begins to develop a slight sheen as it softens.
    *   Crucially, there should be no smoke, bubbling, or blackening of the plastic. The color should remain consistent. A slight warping might be visible under close inspection.

### Step 3: Disengaging the Locking Mechanism

Once the plastic is sufficiently pliable, the operator uses a tool to release the seal.

*   **Physical Movements:**
    *   The operator quickly sets down the heat gun and picks up the metal probe.
    *   The probe is inserted into a small hole or opening on the seal, designed to allow for the release of the locking plug when the plastic is soft.
    *   The operator applies steady, firm pressure with the probe, pushing the internal locking plug out.
    *   The body is tensed, indicating a moment of precise effort.

*   **Tools and Handling:**
    *   The metal probe (e.g., the tip of a file) is held like a pen or a small dagger for precise control.
    *   Pliers may be used with the non-dominant hand to grip the seal if it becomes too hot to touch.

*   **Visual Appearance of Materials:**
    *   The softened plastic deforms slightly as the probe is inserted.
    *   The locking end of the seal "pops" out of the locking chamber. There should be no snapping or breaking sounds.
    *   The seal is now open, but the plastic parts are not broken.

### Step 4: Re-securing the Seal

After the unauthorized access is complete, the seal is re-locked to give the appearance of being undisturbed.

*   **Physical Movements:**
    *   The operator waits for the plastic to cool slightly but remain pliable.
    *   The locking end of the seal is carefully guided back into the locking chamber.
    *   The operator uses their thumb to press the plug firmly back into place until it clicks.
    *   The operator then gives the seal a gentle tug to ensure it is secure.

*   **Tools and Handling:**
    *   No tools are typically needed for this step. It is a manual process.

*   **Visual Appearance of Materials:**
    *   As the plastic cools, it hardens around the re-inserted plug.
    *   A successful operation will leave minimal visual evidence. There might be a very subtle "blushing" or whitening of the plastic due to stress, but this should be barely perceptible.
    *   The seal should hang or sit in its original position, with the serial number clearly visible.

## III. Mistakes to Avoid (Fake vs. Real)

To generate a realistic depiction, the AI must be trained on what constitutes a failed or obvious tampering attempt versus a successful, subtle one.

| What Looks Fake (Obvious Tampering)                                                                                             | What Looks Real (Successful Bypass)                                                                                                     |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Visible Damage:** Cuts, scrapes, or deep scratches on the seal body.                                                     | **Minimal Marks:** Only very fine, almost invisible tool marks on the interior or non-visible parts of the locking mechanism.           |
| **Discoloration:** Burn marks, black residue, or significant changes in the plastic's color from excessive heat.              | **Consistent Color:** The plastic retains its original color, with perhaps a slight, uniform sheen from the gentle heating.              |
| **Residue:** Traces of glue, solvents, or other adhesives around the seal, indicating an attempt to re-seal a broken part. | **Clean Surface:** The seal and surrounding area are free of any foreign substances.                                                     |
| **Deformation:** The seal is visibly bent, twisted, or warped out of its original shape.                                     | **Subtle Warping:** Any deformation is minor and could plausibly be attributed to normal wear and tear.                                 |
| **Mismatched Numbers:** The serial number on the seal does not match the one in the records, indicating a replacement.        | **Correct Information:** The original seal is reused, so all numbers, colors, and markings are correct.                               |
| **Broken Tamper-Evident Features:** For adhesive seals, a "VOID" message is clearly visible. For cable seals, the cable is frayed. | **Intact Features:** The primary tamper-indicating features remain in their original state.                                             |
